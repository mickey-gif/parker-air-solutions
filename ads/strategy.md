# Parker Air Solutions — Winter Install Campaign Strategy
## Q2 2026 (April–June)

---

## Core Idea (Non-Negotiable)

> **Winter is the best time to install air conditioning.**

Every element of this campaign — every ad, every headline, every landing page section, every CTA — must serve this single idea. No side messages. No energy savings tangents. No brand story. One idea, hammered from every angle.

The villain is not Parker Air's competitors. The villain is **timing** — specifically, waiting until summer.

---

## Strategic Logic

Brisbane homeowners, renovators, and small business owners mentally file "install aircon" under summer tasks. That conditioning is the enemy. The job of this campaign is to break that assumption and replace it with one truth: **the people who wait until summer are the ones who suffer most in summer.**

This is not a discount campaign. This is a reframe campaign. The offer is not a price cut — it is **access, availability, and readiness**. Parker Air can install your system properly, now, while the rest of Brisbane is still asleep on the idea. Come summer, you'll be the only one not sweating in a queue.

---

## Pain Points (All Roads Lead to the Core Idea)

Every pain point below is a direct consequence of NOT installing in winter. Each one is the setup; the core idea is the punchline.

### Pain Point 1 — The Summer Queue
When the first 35°C day hits Brisbane, every HVAC company in South East Queensland goes from available to booked out in 48 hours. Wait times stretch 3–6 weeks. You made the call too late. You're already behind 200 other people who also left it too long.

**The solution the core idea provides:** Book in winter and you're first in line. Installers are available. You pick your date. You're done before the heat arrives.

---

### Pain Point 2 — Summer Pricing
Peak season demand inflates quotes. When installers are turning away work, they price accordingly. Rushing to book in November doesn't just cost you time — it costs you money.

**The solution the core idea provides:** Winter is off-peak. Parker Air's schedule has room, which means competitive, honest pricing — not desperation rates.

---

### Pain Point 3 — Rushed Installations Mean Problems
When technicians are running back-to-back jobs across Brisbane in 38°C heat, shortcuts happen. Rushed installs lead to callbacks, warranty issues, and systems that underperform from day one.

**The solution the core idea provides:** Winter bookings get careful, unhurried installations. Parker Air runs proper commissioning. Your Mitsubishi or Daikin system goes in right, the first time.

---

### Pain Point 4 — You're Losing Winter Comfort Right Now
A reverse-cycle air conditioner doesn't just cool — it's one of the most efficient ways to heat a home. Every cold week you spend without one is money spent on electric heaters or gas, comfort you're not getting, and energy bills that don't need to be that high.

**The solution the core idea provides:** Install now and start using it as a heater this winter. By the time summer arrives, you know the system, you've used it, and it's already delivering.

---

### Pain Point 5 — First Hot Day = Too Late
The moment the temperature spikes, the regret hits. You meant to sort it. You told yourself you would. Now you're making calls, being told "earliest availability is 4 weeks," and spending another summer wishing you'd moved sooner.

**The solution the core idea provides:** People who book in winter never experience this moment. Their system is installed, tested, and ready before the heat is even a thought.

---

## What This Campaign Is NOT About

These topics must not appear in any ad, landing page section, or CTA. They dilute the core idea and split attention.

- Energy ratings or star labels
- Brand comparisons (Mitsubishi vs Daikin vs others)
- Parker Air's history or company story
- Ducted vs split system education
- Government rebates or finance schemes
- Maintenance schedules or service plans
- General "why aircon is good" messaging

If any of the above shows up in a brief, cut it.

## Campaign Architecture (Meta)

### Funnel Structure

```
COLD (Prospecting) — Awareness → Interruption
  ↓
WARM (Retargeting) — Video viewers, page visitors, engagers
  ↓
HOT (Conversion) — Lead form clicks, landing page visitors, direct inquiries
```

### Campaign 1 — Prospecting (Cold Audiences)

**Objective:** Conversions (Lead event)
**Structure:** CBO (Campaign Budget Optimisation)
**Audiences:**
- Ad Set A: Brisbane homeowners 30–60, home improvement interests, property ownership signals
- Ad Set B: SEQ renovators — home renovation, building & construction, home décor interests
- Ad Set C: Small business owners in SEQ — business owner behaviour, commercial property interests

**Exclusions:** Existing leads, past website converters, page fans (move to retargeting)
**Placements:** Automatic (skew toward Feed and Reels)
**Optimisation event:** Lead (form submission on landing page)
**Attribution:** 7-day click, 1-day view

### Campaign 2 — Retargeting (Warm Audiences)

**Objective:** Conversions (Lead event)
**Structure:** ABO (tighter budget control per ad set)
**Audiences:**
- Video viewers (50%+) from prospecting ads — last 30 days
- Instagram/Facebook page engagers — last 60 days
- Website visitors who did not convert — last 30 days (requires pixel)

**Spend split:** 30% of total budget toward retargeting

## Creative Strategy

The creative must feel like it's speaking to someone who hasn't thought about this yet — not someone already shopping. The tone is: friendly provocation. Not fear. Not panic. The quiet, knowing reminder from someone who's already sorted.

### Ad Format Priority (in order)

1. **Short-form video (15–30s)** — Primary format. Either UGC-style (honest, direct-to-camera) or b-roll with VO narrative (Parker Air's PA-P07 format). Hook must land in the first 3 seconds.
2. **Single image with strong headline** — Secondary. High-contrast, bold copy. Drives to landing page.
3. **Carousel** — Used in retargeting only. Each card addresses one pain point in sequence, ending on CTA.

### Hook Strategy (First 3 Seconds)

All hooks must enter with the problem of waiting, not with Parker Air. Examples:

- *"Everyone installs aircon in summer. That's exactly why you shouldn't."*
- *"Brisbane's best HVAC installers are booked 5 weeks out every November. Here's how to not be that person."*
- *"Your aircon should already be in. Here's why."*
- *"Most people wait until the first hot day. By then, it's too late."*

### Body Copy Structure (all formats)

```
Hook → Agitate the timing mistake → Reveal the winter advantage → Social proof → CTA
```

Never introduce a new topic in the body. Every sentence must be a reason why winter is better for installation.

### CTA

Single CTA across all ads: **"Book Your Free Quote"**

Not "Learn More." Not "Get a Price." Not "Contact Us." The CTA frames the action as low-friction (free) and high-intent (quote = buying intent).

## Landing Page Brief

*(Full build covered in Phase 2 — this section covers strategic requirements only.)*

### One Job Only

The landing page has one conversion goal: get the visitor to submit a quote request. Everything on the page must support that action and the core idea.

### Above the Fold

- Headline locked on the core idea: *"Winter Is the Best Time to Install Aircon"* (or variant)
- Sub-headline: names the villain (waiting until summer) and the promise (availability, honest pricing, ready before the heat)
- CTA button: "Book Your Free Quote — Takes 60 Seconds"
- Trust signal: Google star rating + number of reviews
- Local anchor: Brisbane / South East Queensland named in the first 100 words

### Social Proof Placement

Google Reviews embedded immediately after first scroll. Real names, real reviews. No stock photos.

### Page Flow (Top to Bottom)

1. Hero — core idea headline + CTA + trust bar
2. Pain point block — the 5 pain points, framed as "Why people regret waiting until summer"
3. How it works — simple 3-step process (Quote → Book → Install), no more
4. Proof — Google reviews + real team/job photos (Matt's shoots)
5. CTA repeat — "Still thinking? Your summer self will thank you." → quote form

### What Must NOT Be on the Page

- Brand story section
- Full service menu (split vs ducted vs multi-split)
- Energy efficiency education
- Any content that distracts from the single conversion goal

## Audience Personas (Targeting Reference)

### Persona 1 — The Homeowner (Primary)
- Brisbane/SEQ, 30–55, owns home, family household
- Has thought about aircon before but always put it off
- Pain point: will scramble in November and regret it
- Motivation: comfort at home, sorted before summer

### Persona 2 — The Renovator
- Mid-reno or post-reno, 35–60
- Installing or upgrading while walls are open anyway
- Pain point: summer rush means a botched installation on a fresh renovation
- Motivation: clean, proper install while there's time to do it right

### Persona 3 — The Small Business Owner
- SEQ, owns or leases commercial space
- Operations depend on comfortable working environment
- Pain point: losing productivity when system fails in summer heat
- Motivation: reliable system in before summer = zero disruption
