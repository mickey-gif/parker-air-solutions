## VIDEO 1: Case Study — Today's Job Walkthrough

**Framework:** Project Case Study

**Final Concept:** Zac walks through the job as it's happening — what they're installing, which unit, why it's the right choice for this home, and what features it has. Features-led, not transformation-led. No before/after needed. Zac is the expert guide, B-roll carries the visual story. Short scripted prompts — one sentence per beat.

**Script / Outline:**

- **Act 1 — Hook/Context:** Zac on camera next to the unit (boxed or mid-install). *"Today we're out in [suburb] putting in a [brand] split — here's what we're working with."*
- **Act 2 — The Walkthrough:** Zac points out key features as B-roll covers it. Talking points (adapt to whatever unit is going in):
  - What size/kilowatt and why it suits this room/home
  - Key feature of the unit (e.g. inverter, quiet operation, wifi control, energy rating)
  - One thing the homeowner will notice straight away
- **Act 3 — Wrap:** *"Clean install, right unit for the job. That's what we do."* Unit powers on. Parker Air logo.

**Hooks:**
- *Source:* Authority/Guides Scripting Hook + Action-Based Visual Hook
- *Visual Hook:* Zac standing next to the unit mid-install, hand on it — ownership, confidence
- *Verbal Hook:* "Today we're out in [suburb] — here's what we're putting in and why."
- *Written Hook:* "Today's job 👇"

**Shot List:**
- Zac on camera — 3 scripted prompts (unit intro, 1–2 features, wrap line)
- Unit being unboxed or mounted (B-roll to cover Act 2)
- Close-up of unit branding/model label
- Refrigerant lines being run
- Indoor unit going up on wall
- Unit powering on — first run (60fps)
- Wide of clean finished install

**Editor Notes:**
- *Pacing:* Conversational walkthrough pace — not rushed. Under 60 seconds.
- *Visuals:* Text callouts for the features Zac mentions (e.g. "5-star energy rating", "WiFi enabled") — editor adds these as animated labels
- *Music:*
  - Genre: Confident Hip-Hop / Modern Corporate
  - Energy/Mood: Competent, knowledgeable, clean
  - BPM: 95–105

## VIDEO 3: The Brothers Debate

**Framework:** Situational Creative

**Final Concept:** Jonah walks up to Zac mid-task with a "fact" he read online — that you only need to service your aircon every 5 years. Zac stops. Turns to camera. Dry look. One-line correction. Jonah looks confused. Cut to stat. Comedy comes entirely from Zac's deadpan reaction — his reserved nature IS the punchline.

**Concept Type:** Setup and Payoff / Expectation vs. Reality

**Skit Outline:**

- **Setup:** Jonah walks into frame while Zac is working. Confident, slightly smug: *"Oi, I read online you only need to service your aircon every five years."*
- **Punchline beat:** Zac stops what he's doing. Slowly turns and looks directly into the camera. (Hold for 2 seconds. No words.)
- **Written overlay:** "This guy…"
- **Resolution:** Zac, still looking at camera: *"Every year. Minimum."* Turns back to work.
- **Jonah:** *"...What?"*
- **Hard cut to stat card:** "Dirty filters reduce efficiency by up to 40%. Service annually. 📞 Parker Air Solutions."

**Hooks:**
- *Source:* Direct Address + Problem/Mistake Scripting Hook + Action-Based Visual Hook
- *Visual Hook:* Jonah walking confidently into frame — energy immediately signals something's about to go wrong
- *Verbal Hook:* "I read online you only need to service your aircon every five years."
- *Written Hook:* "This guy…" (on Zac's reaction)

**Shot List:**
- Wide: Zac working, Jonah enters frame from side
- Medium: Jonah delivers his line
- Medium/CU: Zac's slow turn and look to camera (THIS IS THE MONEY SHOT — get multiple takes)
- Medium: Zac delivers correction, turns back
- Medium: Jonah's confused reaction
- Static card: stat + CTA (designed in post)

**Editor Notes:**
- *Pacing:* The pause on Zac's look needs to breathe — don't cut it short. 1.5–2 full seconds of silence before the "This guy…" text drops. That pause IS the joke.
- *Sound design:* Subtle comedic sound effect on the camera look (tumbleweed, cricket, or just silence — test both)
- *Visuals:* Text "This guy…" drops in on Zac's face, deadpan
- *Music:*
  - Genre: Light comedic / lo-fi
  - Energy/Mood: Casual, playful
  - BPM: 90–100

**Sell strategy (on the day):** Pitch Jonah first — he's already willing. Explain it in one line: *"Jonah says something dumb, Zac does the dry look to camera, done."* Jonah sells Zac. Frame it early: "It's funny because it's a look, not a performance — Zac barely has to do anything."

- *Written Hook:* "Is air con servicing actually worth it?"

**Shot List:**

## VIDEO 7: Dynamic B-Roll — Parker Air at Work

**Framework:** Dynamic B-Roll

**Final Concept:** No talent. Pure process footage of the install and service, cut to beat. Core feeling: competent, clean, professional. The work speaks for itself.

**Core Feeling:** Competent. Clean. Reliable.

**Music Direction:**
- Genre: Upbeat Confident Hip-Hop — no vocals
- Energy/Mood: Smooth but purposeful — the tradesman who knows exactly what they're doing
- BPM: 100–110
- Build up toward the unit powering on as the visual payoff

**Beat Map (rough):**
- 0–4s: Build-up — tools, arriving at job, gear unloaded
- 4–12s: Install in progress — outdoor unit, refrigerant lines, indoor unit going up
- 12–18s: Service in progress — filter out, clean, drain clear
- 18–22s: Beat drop / payoff — unit powers on, cool air, display screen lights up
- 22–28s: Match cut exterior unit → interior display screen lighting up
- 28–30s: Parker Air logo fade

**Shot List (capture at 60fps throughout):**
- Tools laid out / being picked up (macro/close)
- Outdoor unit being mounted on brackets — wide + close
- Refrigerant lines being run along wall
- Indoor head unit being levelled on wall
- Filter being removed and cleaned (dirty → clean contrast)
- Drain pan being cleared
- Indoor unit powering on — first cool air blast (MONEY SHOT — 60fps)
- Interior display screen lighting up (speed ramp)
- Match cut candidate: looking up at outdoor unit → looking up at indoor ceiling unit
- Wide of finished clean install on wall
- Team working (incidental — no need to pose)

**Editor Notes:**
- *All cuts on the beat*
- *Speed ramp on the unit powering on* — slow to emphasise the moment
- *Match cut:* outdoor unit → indoor display screen is the creative centrepiece
- *No text overlays* — logo at the very end only
- *Visuals:* Shoot variety — wide, medium, macro. Never hold one angle too long.

## VIDEO 9: Crew Verdict — "One word for today's job"

**Framework:** Situational Creative (Recurring Format)

**Final Concept:** At the end of the day, ask each crew member one question: *"One word to describe today's job."* Jonah goes first (energy), Zac second (dry), Laurence last (shy pause = the funniest moment). Stack the three answers fast. 15 seconds total. Recurring — new word, same format, every shoot.

**Script / Outline:**

- **Text card opens:** *"End of day. One word. Go."*
- **Jonah:** [Something enthusiastic — "unreal", "mint", "hectic"]
- **Zac:** [One dry word — "good." Full stop. Turns away.]
- **Laurence:** [Long awkward pause... camera holds... then one quiet word. The pause is the joke.]
- Fast cut to Parker Air logo.

**Hooks:**
- *Source:* Direct Address Scripting Hook + Action-Based Visual Hook
- *Visual Hook:* Fast-cut montage of three faces — quick, punchy
- *Verbal Hook:* "End of day. One word. Go."
- *Written Hook:* "We asked the crew…"

**Shot List:**
- Film each person separately — medium shot, natural light, end of day
- Capture genuine reactions — don't over-direct Laurence, his natural awkward pause IS the content
- Film Zac last if possible so his dry energy lands as the "before" Laurence's pause

**Editor Notes:**
- *Pacing:* Fast cuts between each answer. Jonah → Zac → Laurence. Hold on Laurence's pause — don't cut it. That's the laugh.
- *Sound design:* Silence on Laurence's pause. No music underneath that moment.
- *Visuals:* Name text under each person on first appearance (establishes who they are for the series)
- *Music:*
  - Genre: Upbeat Lo-fi / Light Hip-Hop
  - Energy/Mood: Fun, casual, end-of-day energy

## VIDEO 10: 5 Signs Your Split Needs a Service

**Framework:** Engaging Educational (No talent — B-roll + animated text)

**Final Concept:** Five signs on screen, one by one, each paired with relevant B-roll. No talent required. Pure educational content with a service CTA at the end. Evergreen — can be reposted seasonally.

**Script (on-screen text — animated in one by one):**

1. *"It's not cooling as well as it used to"*
2. *"There's a smell when it runs"*
3. *"You can hear rattling or unusual noise"*
4. *"The airflow feels weak"*
5. *"It's been more than 12 months since your last service"*

**End card:** *"If any of these sound familiar — book your service. parkerairsolutions.com.au"*

**Hooks:**
- *Source:* Problem/Mistake Scripting Hook + Transition Hook Visual Hook
- *Visual Hook:* Close-up of a visibly dirty filter — immediate recognition for homeowners
- *Verbal Hook:* None (no talent) — written hook carries it
- *Written Hook:* "5 signs your split system needs a service 👇"

**Shot List:**
- CU: dirty filter (hook shot)
- Unit running but clearly struggling — warm air / weak airflow if achievable
- CU: visible mould or buildup in drain pan (sign 2)
- Any unusual component that might rattle (sign 3)
- Hand held near vent — weak airflow (sign 4)
- Calendar graphic or unit with dust (sign 5) — can be a graphic in post if not achievable on site
- Clean filter reinstalled + unit running well (payoff B-roll)

**Editor Notes:**
- *Pacing:* Each sign animates in, holds for 1.5 seconds, B-roll matches. Total video under 30 seconds.
- *Visuals:* Clean white text on B-roll. Number (1, 2, 3...) animates first, then the text. Satisfying.
- *End card:* Parker Air logo + website URL
- *Music:*
  - Genre: Light Upbeat / Clean Corporate
  - Energy/Mood: Helpful, calm, informative
