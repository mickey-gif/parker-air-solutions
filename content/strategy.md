## Brand Voice

Parker Air Solutions is built on three things: show up on time, fix it properly, tell the truth. No upsell. No bull.

The content voice mirrors Zac — direct, no fluff, quietly confident. It doesn't try to entertain, it just shows competence and lets the work speak. Where entertainment comes in, it comes from Jonah and the brother dynamic — never from trying to make Zac something he's not.

---

## Talent Profiles

### Zac Parker — Owner / Lead Tech / Face of the Business

- Head down, keep busy type. Not a talker or performer.
- Monotone on camera — dry, flat, but reads as knowledgeable and trustworthy
- Kind with customers but no fluff — no buttering up, no push. Just there to work.
- Won't open up or self-promote
- Stresses over small things going wrong but NEVER gives up — always finds a solution. Won't leave a job unfinished. **This is the brand.**
- **Content rules:** Short scripted lines only. Walk-up mid-task format removes performance pressure. His dryness is the asset. Never ask him to be enthusiastic. Never write "bullshit" — use "bull" instead.

### Jonah Parker — Zac's Brother / Assistant

- The joker — always smiling, loves winding Zac up
- The only person who can get a genuine reaction out of Zac on camera
- Assistant role — Zac is the knowledge, Jonah gets told what to do and does it well
- Easy to film, takes direction well, not good with too much information
- Generally curious and interested — can speak to anything at a surface level
- **Content rules:** Give him the vibe, not the script. 2–3 bullet points max. He's the energy and the unlock for Zac. Jonah vouching for Zac lands stronger than Zac vouching for himself.

### Laurence — Apprentice (~6–8 months)

- English is his second language — this is why he's quiet, not disinterest
- Doesn't initiate conversation — always one-way, won't talk unless spoken to directly
- Not invested in content delivery — it's not his business
- Gravitates toward Jonah, comes across as Jonah's support
- **Content rules:** NEVER give him lines or complex scripts. One-word formats only. His pause before answering — likely finding the right word in English — is charming on camera. Don't cut it. Best in reaction shots and brief unscripted moments.

## Recurring Content Formats

### 1. The Brothers Debate
**Who:** Zac + Jonah
**Format:** Jonah delivers a wrong "fact" he read online with full confidence. Zac stops, turns, looks directly at camera (dead-eyed, 2 full seconds). Corrects in one line. Jonah looks confused. Cuts to a stat card.
**Why it works:** Zac's natural dryness is the punchline — he doesn't have to perform. Jonah does all the energy work. Ends with an educational stat that makes it shareable.
**Cadence:** Once per shoot. New topic each time. Build a library of wrong facts to use.

**Topic bank:**
- "You only need to service every 5 years" *(used: Apr 9 shoot)*
- "You should leave your aircon on 24/7 to save power"
- "All aircon brands are basically the same"
- "You don't need to clean the filters if it's still blowing cold"
- "Ducted is always better than splits"

---

### 2. Crew Verdict — One Word
**Who:** Jonah + Zac + Laurence
**Format:** End of each shoot day. Ask each crew member one question: "One word to describe today's job." Film separately. Stack fast. Jonah (enthusiastic) → Zac (dry one word) → Laurence (long pause, quiet word).
**Why it works:** Three distinct personalities, contrast is the joke. Laurence's pause builds audience affection over time. Recurring series — audiences will start waiting for Laurence's answer specifically.
**Cadence:** Every shoot. New word each time. Builds as an ongoing series.

---

### 3. Walk-Up Hot Takes
**Who:** Zac (primary) or Jonah (simple topics)
**Format:** Adrian walks up to crew member mid-task. Asks one pointed question off camera. They answer without stopping work. Short, factual, unscripted.
**Why it works:** No performance required — they just react. Zac's dry delivery reads as expert authority. The more confrontational the question, the better the answer.
**Cadence:** 2–3 per shoot. Rotate topics. Always walk-up — never ask them to stop and face camera.

**Topic bank:**
- Is air con servicing actually worth it?
- Splits or ducted — which is better?
- Why does my air con smell?
- Why does my aircon freeze up?
- What happens if I don't service my aircon?
- How long should a split system last?
- Is a more expensive unit actually worth it?
- What's the most common mistake people make with their aircon?
- Why does my aircon make a noise when it turns on?
- Is WiFi-enabled aircon worth it?

---

### 4. Case Study — Today's Job
**Who:** Zac
**Format:** Zac walks through the job as it's happening — what's being installed, why this unit, what features matter for this home. Features-led. Editor adds animated text callouts for specs.
**Why it works:** Shows expertise without requiring Zac to sell himself. The unit and the work do the selling.
**Cadence:** Once per shoot. Adapt to whatever job is happening that day.

---

### 5. Dynamic B-Roll
**Who:** No talent
**Format:** Process footage throughout the day cut to beat. Core feeling: competent, clean, reliable.
**Signature edit:** Outdoor unit → indoor display screen lighting up. Match cut. Speed ramp on first power-on.
**Cadence:** Every shoot. Shoot at 60fps throughout. No dedicated block needed.

---

### 6. BTS — Day on the Tools
**Who:** Zac (VO recorded post-shoot)
**Format:** Phone footage all day, candid and unposed. Zac records 3-line VO after rough cut is assembled — VO to picture, not the other way around.
**Cadence:** Every shoot. Start phone footage the moment the crew arrives.

## Strategic Angles (Underused — Future Opportunities)

- **"Won't leave until it's fixed"** — Zac's obsessive problem-solving as a content series. Jobs that go sideways, Zac figures it out. Jonah narrates or reacts. No performance required from Zac.
- **Jonah as brand ambassador** — Jonah talking about Zac's work lands more credibly than Zac talking about his own. Test a brand promise video where Jonah delivers the lines instead.
- **Laurence's journey** — apprentice learning on the job. As his English and confidence builds, his arc becomes content. Document it early.
- **Marine AC** — Parker Air are marine AC experts, which is a strong differentiator. Dedicated shoot on a boat or marina when opportunity arises.

## Sell Strategy for Skits (Trade Clients)

Parker Air crew will initially resist any skit content — the "cringe" objection is really a social cost calculation about what their mates will think.

**What works:**
1. Pitch Jonah first — he's already willing. He sells Zac.
2. Explain the mechanic in one sentence: *"Jonah says something dumb, Zac does the look, done."*
3. Frame it early: *"It's funny because it's a look, not a performance. Zac barely has to do anything."*
4. Step back and let them convince each other.
