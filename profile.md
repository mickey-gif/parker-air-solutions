## Brand Voice & Tone

Direct, no fluff, quietly confident. Parker Air doesn't butter customers up and doesn't push. They show up on time, fix it properly, and tell the truth about what you need. No upsell. No bull.

Content should mirror Zac's natural style — dry, factual, trustworthy. Entertainment comes from the brother dynamic (Jonah), not from trying to make Zac perform.

## Talent — On-Camera Profiles

### Zac Parker (owner)
- Head down, keep busy — not a talker or performer
- Monotone, dry, flat delivery — reads as knowledgeable and trustworthy on camera
- Kind but no fluff — just there to work, always serious
- Won't open up or self-promote
- Key character detail: stresses over small things but NEVER gives up — always finds a solution
- **Content rules:** Short scripted lines only. Walk-up mid-task format. His dryness is the asset. Never write "bullshit" — use "bull". Never ask him to be enthusiastic.

### Jonah Parker (Zac's brother — assistant)
- The joker — always smiling, loves winding Zac up
- The only person who can get a genuine reaction out of Zac
- Assistant role — Zac is the knowledge, Jonah takes direction and does it well
- Easy to film, takes direction well, can't handle too much information at once
- **Content rules:** Give him the vibe, not the script. 2–3 bullet points max. He's the energy and the unlock for Zac.

### Laurence (apprentice — ~6–8 months)
- English is his second language — primary reason for quietness, not disinterest
- Won't initiate conversation — always one-way
- Not invested in content delivery — not his business
- Gravitates toward Jonah
- **Content rules:** NEVER give him lines or scripts. One-word formats only. His pause finding the right word in English is charming — don't cut it. Reaction shots and brief unscripted moments only.

## Recurring Content Formats

### Brothers Debate (skit)
Jonah delivers a wrong "fact" confidently. Zac stops, turns, stares at camera (2 seconds, dead-eyed). Corrects in one line. Jonah looks confused. Cuts to stat card. Zac's dryness is the punchline.

**Topic bank (unused):**
- "You should leave your aircon on 24/7 to save power"
- "All aircon brands are basically the same"
- "You don't need to clean filters if it's still blowing cold"
- "Ducted is always better than splits"

### Crew Verdict
End of shoot day. One question: "One word to describe today's job." Jonah (enthusiastic) → Zac (dry) → Laurence (long pause, quiet word). Stack fast. Recurring series.

### Walk-Up Hot Takes
Adrian walks up to crew member mid-task. One pointed question off camera. They answer without stopping. No performance required.

**Topic bank:**
- Why does my aircon freeze up?
- What happens if I don't service my aircon?
- How long should a split system last?
- Is a more expensive unit actually worth it?
- What's the most common mistake people make with their aircon?
- Why does my aircon make a noise when it turns on?
- Is WiFi-enabled aircon worth it?
